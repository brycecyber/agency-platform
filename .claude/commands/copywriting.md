---

## THE CORE TRANSFORMATION

Every piece of scraped copy can be upgraded using one rule:
**"Features tell. Benefits sell. Outcomes close."**

| Level | Example | Quality |
|-------|---------|---------|
| Feature (weak) | "We do plumbing repairs" | Forgettable |
| Benefit (better) | "We fix your leak fast so your home stays dry" | Good |
| Outcome (best) | "Austin families sleep easy knowing we answer at 3am" | Converts |

Always push to the outcome level for headlines and CTAs.

## THE 5-PART HERO FORMULA (Apply to Every Site)

```
1. H1 Headline     → Outcome + City (what they get + where)
2. Subheadline     → Proof + Urgency (why you, why now)
3. Primary CTA     → Action verb + Benefit ("Get My Free Estimate")
4. Secondary CTA   → Lower commitment ("See Our Work")
5. Trust badges    → 3 specific signals (rating, years, license)
```

### Example (Plumber)
```
H1: Austin's Most Trusted Plumber — Available 24/7
Sub: Serving 500+ Austin families since 2009. Same-day service. Upfront pricing. No surprises.
CTA1: 📞 Call Now: (512) 555-0100
CTA2: Get a Free Quote →
Trust: ⭐ 4.9 (312 Reviews) | Licensed & Insured | Same-Day Service
```

## COPYWRITING FRAMEWORKS

### PAS (Problem → Agitation → Solution)
Best for: Hero subtext, service descriptions, FAQ answers

```
Problem:    "Dealing with a clogged drain is frustrating..."
Agitation:  "...and if ignored, it can back up into your home and cause thousands in damage."
Solution:   "We clear any drain in under an hour — guaranteed or you don't pay."
```

### AIDA (Attention → Interest → Desire → Action)
Best for: Full hero sections, landing page flow

```
Attention:  Big bold headline that speaks to a specific pain or desire
Interest:   Subtext that reveals your USP and proof
Desire:     Social proof + trust badges + urgency
Action:     Dominant CTA button with benefit-driven text
```

### BAB (Before → After → Bridge)
Best for: About sections, service descriptions, why-choose-us

```
Before: "Before working with us, homeowners dreaded unexpected repair bills."
After:  "Now they have a trusted partner who gives honest quotes and keeps their word."
Bridge: "We built our reputation in [City] by doing the one thing others don't: showing up on time and telling the truth about what the job will cost."
```

## SERVICE DESCRIPTION FORMULA

Every service card needs 3 elements:

```
1. Service name (what it is)
2. Pain it solves (why they need it)
3. Differentiator (why from you specifically)
```

### Examples

**Drain Cleaning:**
- ❌ Weak: "Professional drain cleaning services"
- ✅ Strong: "Clogged or slow drains? We clear blockages fast with camera inspection — so you know exactly what's wrong and exactly what it costs before we start."

**HVAC Tune-Up:**
- ❌ Weak: "Annual HVAC maintenance"
- ✅ Strong: "Prevent a $3,000 breakdown with a $99 tune-up. We check 21 points on your system and tell you honestly if anything needs attention — no upselling."

**Roof Inspection:**
- ❌ Weak: "Free roof inspections"
- ✅ Strong: "Had a storm recently? Get a free inspection before filing a claim. We document everything and work directly with your insurance company."

## ABOUT SECTION COPYWRITING

### The Formula
```
Paragraph 1: Origin story (when, who, why)
Paragraph 2: What makes them different (the real reason to choose them)
Paragraph 3: Community commitment / values
```

### Example (Plumber, Austin TX, founded 2009)

**Paragraph 1 (Origin):**
"Smith Plumbing was founded in 2009 by Mike Smith, a licensed master plumber who grew up in Austin and spent 15 years watching homeowners get overcharged by out-of-town companies. He started this company with one truck, one rule: charge what's fair and show up when you say you will."

**Paragraph 2 (Differentiator):**
"Today, we're a team of 8 licensed plumbers serving greater Austin. What sets us apart isn't our equipment — it's our guarantee: we'll tell you the full price before we touch a single pipe. No surprises. If we can't give you a fixed quote, we'll tell you why in plain English."

**Paragraph 3 (Community):**
"We've built our reputation the old-fashioned way — one job at a time, one neighbor at a time. Over 500 Austin families have trusted us, and we take that seriously."

## TESTIMONIAL WRITING (When Real Ones Aren't Available)

Write 3 testimonials that feel specific, not generic. Use this structure:

```
Format: "Specific situation → Specific outcome → Personal detail"
Name: Real-sounding first name + last initial
Location: Suburb or neighborhood + "homeowner" or "resident"
```

### Good Testimonials (Specific)
- "Had a pipe burst at 11pm on a Sunday. [Business] had someone here by midnight, fixed it in under 2 hours, and the price was exactly what they quoted on the phone. Nothing like the last plumber I used." — **Sarah K., Round Rock homeowner**

- "Called 3 HVAC companies before these guys. They were the only ones who gave me a straight answer on what was actually wrong with my unit — and didn't try to sell me a whole new system I didn't need. Saved me $2,400." — **James T., South Austin**

- "I was nervous about the cost, but they had me in and out in 45 minutes and my tooth feels great. The front desk staff remembered my name when I came back. Little things matter." — **Maria G., Pflugerville patient**

### Bad Testimonials (Generic — Never Use)
- "Great service, highly recommend!" — John S.
- "Very professional, would use again." — Mary K.
- "Excellent work!" — Bob M.

### The Power CTA Formula

[Action verb] + [Specific benefit] + [Risk removal]

## FAQ COPY PRINCIPLES

FAQs aren't just information — they're objection handlers. Each answer should:
1. Directly answer the question (no hedging)
2. Reinforce a trust signal
3. End with a soft push toward action

**Example:**
Q: "How much does a roof inspection cost?"
❌ Weak: "Please contact us for pricing."
✅ Strong: "Roof inspections are completely free — no obligation. We show up, inspect every section of your roof, take photos, and walk you through exactly what we found. You decide if you want to move forward. No pressure, ever."

## WRITING RULES FOR LOCAL BUSINESSES

1. **Use "you" and "your" — not "customers" or "clients"**
   - ❌ "We serve customers across Austin"
   - ✅ "We'll be at your door within 2 hours"

2. **Use the city name in copy (not just metadata)**
   - ❌ "We serve the greater metropolitan area"
   - ✅ "Serving Austin, Round Rock, Cedar Park, and Pflugerville"

3. **Be specific about time and numbers**
   - ❌ "Fast response times"
   - ✅ "We'll be there in 2 hours or your diagnostic is free"

4. **Name the fear, then kill it**
   - ❌ "We offer competitive pricing"
   - ✅ "Worried about surprise charges? We give you the full price before we start. Period."

5. **Sound like a person, not a corporation**
   - ❌ "Our team of professionals utilizes industry-leading techniques"
   - ✅ "Mike and his crew have been doing this for 15 years. They'll treat your home like their own."

## NICHE VOICE GUIDES

### Plumbers / HVAC / Electricians
**Voice:** Straight-talking, dependable, no-nonsense
**Avoid:** Corporate-speak, vague promises
**Use:** "we show up", "honest price", "no BS", "your home", "emergency"
**Tone:** Trusted neighbor, not contractor

### Dentists / Healthcare
**Voice:** Warm, reassuring, confident
**Avoid:** Medical jargon, anything that sounds clinical or cold
**Use:** "comfortable", "gentle", "no judgment", "your smile", "we listen"
**Tone:** Caring friend who happens to be a doctor

### Law Firms
**Voice:** Authoritative, empathetic, fighting-for-you
**Avoid:** Legalese, passive voice, hedging
**Use:** "fight for you", "you deserve", "hold them accountable", "we win or you pay nothing"
**Tone:** Fierce advocate, not bureaucrat

### Landscapers / Outdoor Services
**Voice:** Friendly, nature-positive, hardworking
**Avoid:** Too formal, overly technical
**Use:** "beautiful", "transform", "your yard", "neighbors will notice", "pride"
**Tone:** Craftsperson who loves their work

### Auto Repair
**Voice:** Honest, technically capable, straightforward
**Avoid:** Pushy sales language, unexplained jargon
**Use:** "written estimate", "certified", "no surprises", "we explain everything", "your car"
**Tone:** Trusted mechanic friend

## THE COPY QUALITY BAR

Before finalizing any generated site, every piece of copy must pass this test:

**The "So What?" Test:** After reading any sentence, could a customer ask "so what?" If yes, rewrite it.
- ❌ "We have been in business for 15 years." → So what?
- ✅ "15 years means we've seen every problem twice — and we know exactly how to fix it."

**The "Generic Test":** Could this exact sentence appear on ANY competitor's site? If yes, make it more specific.
- ❌ "We provide quality service at competitive prices." → Generic
- ✅ "We fixed 847 Austin roofs last year. Our price beat 3 competitors on 71% of jobs." → Specific

**The "Anxiety Killer" Test:** Does the copy address the customer's #1 fear for this niche?
- Plumbing fear: Will the price be huge? → Address it with upfront pricing guarantee
- HVAC fear: Will I need a whole new unit? → Address with honest diagnosis promise
- Legal fear: Will this cost me more money? → Address with contingency fee model
- Dental fear: Will it hurt? → Address with comfort technology and gentle approach
